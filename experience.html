Ghalia is a student at Columbia Law School, where she focuses on emerging companies and venture capital, and holds a Master's from Penn Engineering in Computer and Information Technology. She is currently a 3L Fellow at Optimal Counsel, a boutique firm dedicated to advising founders and early-stage companies, and will be joining the firm full time after the bar exam.

She is a Tony Patino Fellow, a Davis Polk Leadership Initiative Scholar, and a Cansbridge Fellow, recognitions that reflect her commitment to leadership, service, and cross-border thinking. She is also a recipient of The Diana Award, one of the highest accolades a young person can receive for social action and humanitarian work.

At Columbia, she has worked through the Entrepreneurship Clinic advising startups on formation, governance, and fundraising, translating complex legal frameworks into clear, actionable guidance for founders building in real time.

Before law school, Ghalia graduated from the University of Alberta with a Bachelor of Commerce and Certificates in Leadership and International Learning. She has spent years coaching debate and working with students on communication, an experience that continues to shape how she thinks about clarity, persuasion, and the discipline of speaking well.
