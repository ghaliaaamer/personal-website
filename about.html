About

Teaching people to think clearly and speak with purpose.

I have spent years teaching debate and working closely with students, helping them organize their thinking, build arguments, and speak with conviction in high-pressure settings. That work shaped how I see communication: not as performance, but as a discipline rooted in clarity, preparation, and genuine connection with an audience.

At Columbia Law School, I have deepened that perspective. Studying emerging companies and venture capital has taught me how much depends on the ability to articulate a vision clearly, whether you are pitching investors, advising a founder, or leading a team through uncertainty. The best communicators are not the loudest. They are the most prepared.

I bring that belief into every talk and workshop I give. Whether I am working with students learning to debate for the first time or professionals looking to sharpen how they present ideas, my goal is the same: help people find their voice and use it with intention.
